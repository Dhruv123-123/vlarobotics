About

Physical AI is only as good as
the tests it fails.

VLA Robotics builds the validation infrastructure that makes embodied AI deployable. We instrument, stress-test, and certify robot policies before they touch the real world — so your team ships faster and fails safely.

Our platform sits between model training and physical deployment, running millions of simulation and real-world episodes to surface edge cases that matter.

Capabilities

Built for the full
deployment stack.

01

Policy Stress Testing

Automated adversarial episode generation targeting distribution shift, sensor noise, and failure recovery across 1M+ rollouts per model version.

02

Real-World Benchmarking

Hardware-in-the-loop validation suites executed across standardised manipulation and locomotion tasks with full telemetry capture.

03

Regression Tracking

Continuous per-commit regression detection so model updates don't silently degrade existing capabilities. Versioned pass/fail certificates.

04

Latency Profiling

End-to-end inference latency and jitter analysis across deployment hardware, from edge TPU to cloud inference, with hard real-time guarantees.

05

Safety Certification

Structured documentation output aligned to ISO 13482 and emerging physical AI safety standards for regulatory submissions.

06

CI/CD Integration

Native integration with your training and deployment pipelines. Gate releases on validation outcomes. Block bad models before they ship.

Process

From checkpoint
to certified.

01

Ingest

Push your model checkpoint via our API or connect your training pipeline directly. We accept all major policy formats — diffusion policies, ACT, VLA, and custom architectures.

02

Simulate

We run your policy across a battery of simulation environments — standard benchmarks plus your own task distributions — at scale, in parallel.

03

Validate

Physical validation on standardised hardware rigs in our facility, or remotely instrumented on your own hardware. Full sensor-level telemetry logging.

04

Certify

Structured pass/fail report with per-capability breakdown, regression deltas, and a machine-readable certificate attached to the model hash.
